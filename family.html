Conclusion

What was my role in the project?

My role involved both the UX and UI stages in the design of the Family Friendly Cooking Web App.

How did I interpret the project brief?

The objective of the brief was to design a responsive web app for recipes that meet the needs of its users and solve the problems they are facing with existing recipe apps. Through research
and testing, I wanted to determine the pain points users experienced with current recipe apps. My job was then to create a user experience that solved those problems and design a
responsive web app that simplifies the lives of the users.

What roadblocks did I encounter as I worked on each deliverable? How did I overcome these roadblocks?

I struggled the most with the overall design of Family Friendly Cooking. I wanted the color scheme, icons, and imagery to be inviting to children and adults alike. It was important to find
balance for the app to be family friendly since both families and individuals would be using the app. This involved asking a lot of questions during testing about colors, patterns, and
images and what worked best for each user.

What insights from my research and testing had the biggest impact on my final design?

During my research and testing, I found my users wanted an app that was easy to use, had a big library, wanted to search by ingredient and cooking appliance, and wanted to see how a recipe
was made. This is what inspired me to have not only the child-friendly noted directions, but video demonstrations and utensil images as well. The testing stage highlighted navigational and
design flaws which improved my eye for design and usability

Did I have to change direction at all?

The user testing opened my eyes to the importance of accessibility. The colors I chose did seem to do well, but some of the shadows I used for the buttons were a bit excessive. I did end
using more whitespace than what I originally thought was needed. I also did a lot of text rearranging so that everything could fit on one screen to minimize scrolling.

Would I do anything different?

I believe that critiquing during the earlier stages would have been a huge help. I went back to earlier design decisions several times. I should not be afraid to ask for testers review my
work because the more testers that I have the more information I can get back. It will also give me back up testers in case there are delays with any participants.

Extra insights

I enjoyed the whole process. Taking on both the UX and UI stages of the project enlightened me to the importance of the whole team collaboration. I believe that if this project was
completed within a team setting, the group support would have been valuable. I really look forward to being among a team of creatives in the future.
